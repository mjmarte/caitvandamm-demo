What is the Embodied Resilience Pain Recovery Method, actually?

Embodied Resilience is a method of coaching designed for long-term pain recovery.

It uses somatic, biomechanical, and cognitive strategies to reconnect to body signals and re-establish a lived experience of safety — never a generic protocol, always individualized to you.

Built on three pillars

How the method works

Pain science / brain–body

Pain neuroscience

Grounded in current pain-science research on how the brain and nervous system generate and sustain pain — and how that can change.

Somatic / interoception

Somatic integration

Reconnecting to your body's signals so safety becomes something you feel, not just something you understand.

Therapeutic movement

Therapeutic movement

Integrating movement that builds strength and a renewed sense of safety in motion.

Common questions

Good things to know

How is this different from talk therapy?
Embodied Resilience blends Pain Reprocessing Therapy with somatic work. Rather than only talking through experiences, we work with the body directly — building a felt sense of safety alongside cognitive understanding.
What does a session actually involve?
Sessions start with grounding, then move into client-driven goals that combine cognitive strategies with movement. The work is collaborative and paced to your nervous system, not a fixed script.
How does it relate to my other therapies?
It's designed to be complementary. How it fits alongside other care varies by individual — many clients find it deepens and accelerates the work they're already doing.
How long until I feel improvement?
Many clients notice initial integration within weeks. A typical commitment is three to six months, which gives the nervous system time to genuinely reorganize.
